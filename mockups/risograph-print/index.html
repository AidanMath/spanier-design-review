NOTE 01

The Riso Press

Risograph prints one ink at a time, drum by drum. Each pass is slightly off — the "misregistration" is a feature, not a flaw. That small slip is what makes riso feel alive.

NOTE 02

Two-Color Blue

This edition uses only blue — deep cobalt on brand cobalt. Overlapping layers create a third tone. The grain is inherent to the ink; we amplified it, not manufactured it.

NOTE 03

Zine Influence

Independent publishers like Hato Press and Nieves prove that the most considered design still fits in a hand. This site takes that energy — print logic, web medium.

What we
print on.

Six plates. Six surfaces. One crew that knows them all. Lukas's painters handle every item below in-house — no subcontracted prep, no inherited mistakes. Two coats over a properly primed surface, every time.

PLATE 01

Interior Painting

Walls, ceilings, trim, doors. Two-coat over a primed surface — eggshell on walls, semi-gloss on trim, flat on ceilings unless you'd rather not. Drop cloths and protected hardware are standard, not extra.

PLATE 02

Exterior Painting

Siding, soffits, fascia, trim, garages. Power-washed, scraped, primed and back-rolled. Alkyd-hybrid on woodwork; elastomeric where the surface needs to bridge a crack.

PLATE 03

Cabinet Refinishing

Kitchens, vanities, built-ins. Doors and drawer fronts come off and finish off-site for an even, dust-free coat. Conversion varnish or pigmented lacquer depending on the cabinetry.

PLATE 04

Heritage / Historic Homes

Older homes, lead-safe practice, color-matching to original paint, plaster repair. EPA RRP certified. Patient detail work on the houses that earned it.

PLATE 05

Decks + Fences

Stain, seal, refresh. Penetrating oils, semi-transparent stains, or solid-body coatings depending on the age of the wood and how much sun it sees.

PLATE 06

Specialty Coatings

Epoxy garage floors, commercial walls, fire-rated coatings, anti-graffiti finishes, kitchen-safe cabinet refresh. The jobs other painters route around.

COMMON QUESTIONS · PRINT EDITION

Common
questions.

The eight things most folks ask before they hire a painter. If yours isn't here, call the studio at 320·250·9100 — Lukas picks up.

Q.01

What areas do you serve?

Central Minnesota — St. Cloud, Sartell, Sauk Rapids, Waite Park, Cold Spring, Avon, Albany, Monticello, Foley, Rice, Becker, Big Lake. Roughly a 60-mile radius from St. Cloud.

Q.02

How much does a typical project cost?

Interior repaints start around $2.50–$4.50/sqft of wall area; full exteriors $4,000–$12,000 depending on size, prep, and condition. We give you a real number after a walkthrough — not a per-hour estimate.

Q.03

Do you give free estimates?

Yes. A walkthrough, photos, measurements, and a written quote — at no cost.

Q.04

Are you licensed and insured?

Yes — licensed and insured in Minnesota, plus EPA RRP certified for lead-safe work on pre-1978 homes.

Q.05

What paint brands do you use?

Sherwin-Williams and Benjamin Moore are the defaults. We'll work with whatever you specify.

Q.06

How long does a typical interior repaint take?

A 1,500 sqft repaint is usually 3–5 working days. Cabinetry runs 5–7 days. Exteriors run 1–2 weeks depending on weather and prep.

Q.07

Do you do commercial work?

Yes — offices, dental and medical, restaurants, retail. Most commercial work is scheduled after-hours or weekends so you don't lose business days.

Q.08

Is there a warranty?

Two-year workmanship warranty in writing. If a coat fails because of how we applied it, we come back and fix it.
