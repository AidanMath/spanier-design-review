Studio — St. Cloud, MN

Paint is not the work.
Color is the work —
the room is the proof.

I started Spanier Painting in St. Cloud in 2014 — just me and a van. Over eleven years it has grown into a small crew that I still personally show up with every day. We don't sub out. We don't rush the prep. Color in Minnesota behaves differently than it does in a showroom: the light shifts hard between November and June, and what looks right on a chip under fluorescent can read cold or flat once it's on a twelve-foot wall in January. We learned to test before we commit.

The work runs from kitchen cabinetry in Sartell to heritage exteriors in Cold Spring to commercial suites in Avon. The standard is the same across all of it: we leave a surface better than we found it, and we leave the room looking like itself — not like it was just painted.

No. 03 · The Process

Four visits,
one standard.

Every project, residential or commercial, moves through the same four stages — a walkthrough, a color decision, the prep + paint, and a final inspection backed by a written two-year warranty.

  1. Step 01

    Walkthrough

    Lukas walks the property with you, takes photos, and measures every surface with iPad LiDAR. Accurate square footage in, an accurate quote out — no per-hour guesses.

  2. Step 02

    Color

    Preview your palette on the actual wall with Sherwin-Williams ColorSnap AR, or bring your own swatch. Sample patches optional. See your color before we touch a wall.

  3. Step 03

    Prep + paint

    Drop cloths down, hardware off, surfaces prepped to spec. Two coats unless otherwise noted. Daily clean-up. Lukas is on every job, every day.

  4. Step 04

    Walk + warranty

    Final inspection together — anything that needs another pass gets one. Then it's signed off, in writing, with a two-year workmanship warranty.

Spanier Painting has been the painter Central Minnesota families recommend by name since 2014. Lukas Spanier started the company at nineteen — a one-man crew, one ladder, a willingness to do the prep no one else wanted to.

Twelve years later, Spanier Painting is a four-painter studio working across the St. Cloud region on everything from heritage Queen Annes to two-day commercial turnarounds.

The crew stays small on purpose. Lukas is on every job, every day. We don't sub the work out and we don't rush the prep — the prep is the work that decides whether a paint job lasts two years or twelve.

No. 08 · Q & A

Asked,
and answered.

The questions Lukas hears on a walkthrough, answered in plain language. Anything missing? Pick up the phone — 320-250-9100.

  1. Q.

    What areas do you serve?

    A.

    Central Minnesota — St. Cloud, Sartell, Sauk Rapids, Waite Park, Cold Spring, Avon, Albany, Monticello, Foley, Rice, Becker, Big Lake. Roughly a 60-mile radius from St. Cloud.

  2. Q.

    How much does a typical project cost?

    A.

    Interior repaints start around $2.50–$4.50 per square foot of wall area; full exteriors $4,000–$12,000 depending on size, prep, and condition. We give you a real number after a walkthrough — not a per-hour estimate.

  3. Q.

    Are you licensed and insured?

    A.

    Yes — licensed and insured in Minnesota, plus EPA RRP certified for lead-safe work on pre-1978 homes.

  4. Q.

    What paint brands do you use?

    A.

    Sherwin-Williams and Benjamin Moore are the defaults. We'll work with whatever you specify.

  5. Q.

    How long does a typical interior repaint take?

    A.

    A 1,500 sqft repaint is usually three to five working days. Cabinetry runs five to seven days. Exteriors run one to two weeks depending on weather and prep.

  6. Q.

    Do you do commercial work?

    A.

    Yes — offices, dental and medical, restaurants, retail. Most commercial work is scheduled after-hours or weekends so you don't lose business days.

  7. Q.

    Is there a warranty?

    A.

    Two-year workmanship warranty in writing. If a coat fails because of how we applied it, we come back and fix it.
