Every room I walk into already has a story — in the light it holds, the trim it was built with, the way the ceilings sit at six or nine feet. My practice is to read that story and write the color in. Not impose it. The palette I recommend is always in conversation with the architecture, the furnishings, the compass orientation of the windows. I opened this studio in 2014 on the conviction that paint — applied carefully, by someone who understands color — is the highest-leverage improvement a home or business can receive. A decade in, that conviction has not changed. These listings are the documented record of that work: room by room, building by building, across Central Minnesota.

Lukas Spanier
Principal · Studio Spanier · Est. 2014

Showing protocol · Four-step engagement

How a viewing unfolds

Every engagement opens with a site visit. From there: a documented color conversation, a precise specification, the work itself, and a two-year workmanship warranty in writing.

  1. Step 01

    Walkthrough

    Lukas walks the property with you. Photos, conditions noted, square-footage measured with iPad LiDAR for accuracy that a tape measure cannot match.

    iPad LiDAR
  2. Step 02

    Color

    Sherwin-Williams ColorSnap AR previews on your wall, or bring your own swatch. Sample patches optional. See your color before we touch a wall.

    ColorSnap AR
  3. Step 03

    Prep + paint

    Drop cloths down, hardware off, surfaces prepped, two coats unless specified otherwise. Daily clean-up. Owner on every job.

    Two-coat default
  4. Step 04

    Closing walk-through

    Final inspection together. Punch list resolved on the spot. Two-year workmanship warranty issued in writing before the keys come back.

    2-yr warranty
